UsageTracker 0.1.1 improves first-run account discovery and fixes provider switches in Settings.

## What's new

- Explains why UsageTracker needs Keychain access before starting the background service or triggering macOS permission prompts.
- Clearly directs users to choose **Always Allow** so background usage refreshes do not repeatedly request permission.
- Scans every supported provider when you select **Find my accounts**.
- Automatically enables discovered accounts and their providers.
- Supports rescanning and reports discovery progress and results.
- Preserves the existing startup behavior for users who have already completed onboarding.

## Fixes

- Fixed provider switches in Settings so the full enable or disable flow now rebuilds app state, refreshes enabled providers, and reloads configuration. The switch no longer remains visually stuck in the off state.

## Installation

UsageTracker requires macOS 14 or newer.

```sh
curl --proto '=https' --tlsv1.2 -fsSL \
  https://github.com/joeymalvinni/usagetracker/releases/latest/download/install.sh | bash
```

The installer verifies published SHA-256 checksums, archive contents, and ad-hoc code-signature integrity. By default it installs:

- `UsageTracker.app` in `~/Applications`
- `usage` in `~/.local/bin`

> [!IMPORTANT]
> This release is ad-hoc signed and is **not notarized by Apple**. macOS may require you to approve UsageTracker manually the first time it opens. Follow the [Open Anyway instructions](https://github.com/joeymalvinni/usagetracker/blob/v0.1.1/docs/troubleshooting.md#opening-the-unnotarized-app) without disabling Gatekeeper globally.
